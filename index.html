Biography

I am a Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Agricultural Economics at Kansas State University. My research studies how agricultural policy design, natural-resource access, and risk management incentives shape producer decisions, agricultural markets, and regional economic outcomes.

My job market paper is "Reference Prices, Bidding, and Enrollment in the Conservation Reserve Program." I also study agricultural coagglomeration around the High Plains Aquifer and risk aggregation in crop insurance demand.

Resource-Driven Agricultural Clusters: Coagglomeration Effects of the High Plains Aquifer.

Kihwan Yu and Nathan P. Hendricks

Draft · Revise and resubmit, American Journal of Agricultural Economics

Abstract

We estimate local economic spillovers by exploiting a large increase in crop production that occurred due to the expansion of irrigation in counties overlying the High Plains Aquifer after World War II. While crop production was directly impacted by access to groundwater, livestock product sales also increased significantly, reaching a 235% increase in sales compared to counties outside the aquifer. The primary spillover to non-farm sectors was an increase in sales in the wholesale sector, perhaps driven by sales of agricultural-related products.
